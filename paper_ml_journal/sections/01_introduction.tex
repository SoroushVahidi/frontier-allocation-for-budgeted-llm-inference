\section{Introduction}
Large language model (LLM) reasoning quality is often evaluated by final-answer accuracy under fixed compute budgets. In this work, we report a conservative, evidence-grounded policy improvement over a strong frontier baseline by combining two gold-free runtime rules (FIX-2 and FIX-4). The promoted policy is \textbf{FIX-2+FIX-4}, validated on disjoint Cohere-only matched-budget evaluations.

The main aggregate evidence contains 720 disjoint examples across three sources (300+120+300), all at budget 6. On this aggregate, FIX-2+FIX-4 achieves 80.69\% versus external baselines L1 (77.64\%), S1 (77.08\%), and TALE (75.14\%), with positive source-stratified confidence-interval lower bounds for all comparisons.

This manuscript intentionally separates promoted findings from exploratory prototypes. FIX-5/FIX-6/FIX-7/FIX-8/FIX-9 are included as negative or non-promoted follow-ups.
