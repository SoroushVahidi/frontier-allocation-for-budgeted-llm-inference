\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

The promoted method, which we term the \textbf{Combined Failure-Trace-Guided Allocator} (FIX-2+FIX-4), is a deterministic precedence policy applied at inference time.  Its two gates---the \textit{Low-Depth Guard} and the \textit{External-Consensus Fallback}---fire in strict priority order; at most one gate acts per example.  All runtime decisions are gold-free: no \texttt{gold\_answer}, \texttt{exact\_match}, or correctness label is accessed.

\subsection{Low-Depth Guard (FIX-2)}
\label{sec:fix2}

The Low-Depth Guard detects frontier answers arising from structurally weak search branches and replaces them with an external majority vote.  The trigger relies exclusively on fields logged during the frontier run: the override reason string and the candidate-pool group count.

\subsection{External-Consensus Fallback (FIX-4)}
\label{sec:fix4}

The External-Consensus Fallback identifies examples where the frontier agreed with its internal reserve selection (\texttt{override\_reason = direct\_frontier\_agree}) yet all three external baselines unanimously chose a different answer.  When this unanimous disagreement holds, the policy substitutes the external consensus answer.
