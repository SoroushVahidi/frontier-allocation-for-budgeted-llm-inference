Equation~(4) should be interpreted as a compact abstraction of the controller objective, not as a fully closed-form optimization solved in isolation. In experiments, we instantiate a deterministic operational policy with explicit state and thresholds (Appendix~X: Operational Controller Specification).

Operationally, a \\emph{branch} is a tracked partial solution state with score, support history, and termination flags; active branches form the frontier. \\emph{Answer groups} are canonicalized output clusters used for support aggregation. \\emph{Branch families} capture root-lineage structure used by repeat/cap controls to reduce collapse. The \\emph{commit rule} triggers only when answer-group support concentration and readiness exceed fixed thresholds while continuation value is sufficiently low. \\emph{Bounded repair} applies deterministic extraction/canonicalization and only limited rescue under explicit consensus conditions.

Accordingly, the controller is fully auditable at implementation level, but includes heuristic and state-dependent gates (e.g., coverage forcing, family caps, anti-collapse interventions) that are not representable as one universal closed-form objective. We therefore treat Equation~(4) as an explanatory abstraction and rely on the appendix-level operational specification for reproducibility-critical detail.
