\paragraph{Limitations.}
Our evidence does not establish a decisive internal ranking between \texttt{strict\_f3} and \texttt{strict\_gate1\_cap\_k6} on the matched manuscript surface; this comparison is fragile/non-decisive across many slices. Likewise, we do not support a universal frontier-family dominance claim over \texttt{external\_l1\_max}; external comparisons are slice-specific and action-budget/matched-substrate bounded. Real-model results are therefore treated as appendix-level robustness corroboration rather than headline proof.

In addition, while the operational controller is reproducible and deterministic at implementation level, several gates are heuristic/state-dependent (e.g., coverage forcing and family-cap logic) rather than globally optimal closed-form control. Finally, held-out analyses include dry-run scaffolds and partial slices; fully exhaustive held-out runs may still be required before making broader external-validity claims.
