Across matched-surface paired/bootstrap/permutation tests (Appendix Table~\ref{tab:claim_safety_statistical_tests}), the Strict-F3 versus Strict-Gate1-Cap-K6 comparison is \textbf{fragile / not statistically decisive}. Signs vary by dataset--budget slice, confidence intervals frequently cross zero, and permutation p-values are mostly non-decisive. We therefore treat these two methods as a statistically close \textbf{top internal frontier-allocation cluster} on the matched surface.

For continuity with prior ablation and diagnostic sections, we use Strict-F3 as a representative controller instantiation. This is a presentation choice, not a claim of universal superiority. The safe conclusion is that the exact internal winner is surface-dependent, while the family-level mechanism claim---that branch-level frontier allocation and early tree-shape control materially affect outcomes---is stable across the core evidence slices.
