% Manuscript insertion: baseline fairness and budget accounting contract
Our external baselines are near-direct, matched-substrate comparators designed to test whether frontier-style allocation provides value under the same \\emph{action-budget} contract. This action budget is the primary fairness unit in the main manuscript comparisons.

Token usage, latency, and cost diagnostics are reported as appendix/accounting evidence and should be interpreted as secondary measurements with coverage limitations. They improve transparency but do not establish full hardware-level systems-cost equivalence across providers or deployment stacks. Therefore, our fairness claim is action-budget comparability under a shared evaluation substrate, not complete compute-cost parity.
