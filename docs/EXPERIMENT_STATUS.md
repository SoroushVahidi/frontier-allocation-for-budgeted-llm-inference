# Experiment status (canonical summary)

## Established infrastructure

- Frontier/controller allocation scaffold is in place.
- Anti-collapse controller mechanisms are implemented and auditable.
- Internal branch-allocation label generation, merge, regime construction, and canonical corpus paths are runnable.
- Matched canonical learning/evaluation passes are runnable.
- Dataset and baseline integration/readiness tooling is established.
- Conservative external process-supervision ingestion is established.

## Strong current assets

- Canonical processed branch-learning corpora now exist with manifests, checksums, schema, and slice summaries.
- Hard-slice instrumentation now covers near-tie, small-margin, adjacent-rank, and exact-promoted-aware pathways.
- External PRM800K integration is now repaired and non-degenerate.
- Multiple matched internal and PRM-assisted evaluation passes now exist with machine-readable summaries.

## Promising directions

- Pairwise and pointwise branch scoring remain among the strongest active learned directions.
- Hard-slice-focused evaluation is now much more informative than earlier global-only summaries.
- PRM-assisted methods are promising enough to keep investigating under the same matched protocol.

## Promising but still mixed outcomes

- Reliability-aware and ambiguity-aware variants remain useful but not settled canonical winners.
- External PRM broad/aligned variants can beat the internal anchor in rebuilt evaluation families, but broad vs aligned still do not separate clearly.
- Comparator-boundary PRM use is diagnostically useful and can produce net-helpful flips, but has not clearly become the main aggregate winner.
- Budget-aware comparator tweaks can be sensible without yet producing a decisive new gain.

## Mixed/negative findings to retain

- Proxy-target mismatch remains a recurring failure mode.
- Robust controller-level superiority over strongest internal anchors is not yet consistently demonstrated across all important slices.
- Exact-promoted held-out evidence remains thinner than desired.
- External uncertainty filtering did not materially solve the main branch-allocation bottleneck.
- Real-model evidence is still narrower than desired for the strongest claims.

## What the current evidence supports

- The repo is now strong enough for a serious paper-facing story built around branch-priority / next-step allocation.
- Internal supervision maturity is much better than before.
- PRM external supervision is real enough to study seriously.
- The remaining challenge is not basic infrastructure but decision-aligned supervision and evaluation on the hardest budget-sensitive slices.

## Not-yet-supported claims

- No robust universal learned-controller winner claim.
- No claim that broad vs aligned PRM usage is already settled.
- No claim that exact-promoted behavior is already well measured.
- No claim that scale or heavier models are the immediate fix.
- No claim that Math-Shepherd should already be the next move.
